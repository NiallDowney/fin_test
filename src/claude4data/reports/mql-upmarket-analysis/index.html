Core Web Lead Analysis: Website Change or Upmarket Shift?

Core web leads (Demo + Contact Sales + Livechat + Trial) · S2 pipeline filtered to core_noncore = 'core' · Sep 2024 – Mar 2026

Over the past 18 months, MQL volume from core web sources has overtaken trial volume — a reversal of the historical pattern. This raises two competing hypotheses: (a) the shift is structural, driven by website and funnel architecture changes that route more visitors toward sales-led paths, or (b) it reflects a genuine move upmarket, where larger companies with bigger budgets prefer to talk to sales. This analysis disentangles the two using lead volume, company size, and S2 pipeline ACV data.

1. Sales-Led vs Self-Serve Split

The most direct measure of whether leads are shifting toward sales-led paths is the ratio of sales-led lead types (Demo + Contact Sales + Livechat) to total core web leads. If the website is funnelling more people toward "talk to us" CTAs, this ratio rises regardless of company size.

Sales-Led Share of Core Web Leads

Sales-led (Demo + Contact Sales + Livechat) / Total core web leads. Ranges 31–44%, currently 37%.

The sales-led share oscillates between 31% and 44% with no sustained upward trend. The spikes in Jan–Feb 2025 and again in Nov 2025 coincide with livechat surges (discussed below) rather than a permanent shift. The current 37% is close to the 18-month average, which argues against a structural rebalancing of the funnel toward sales-led conversion.

So what? If the lead mix were shifting due to upmarket buyers preferring sales paths, we'd expect to see a ratcheting increase in sales-led share. Instead, the ratio mean-reverts — consistent with funnel/website changes that create temporary surges rather than a permanent floor shift.
2. What Drove the Shifts

To understand why the mix fluctuates, we decompose leads by website origin (fin.ai vs intercom.com) and by lead source type. Two structural changes stand out: the launch of fin.ai and the growth of Contact Sales as a conversion path.

fin.ai vs intercom.com Leads

fin.ai appeared mid-2025, now ~34% of core web leads. Pre-Apr 2025 website field unpopulated.

Lead Source Composition

Demo stable. Contact Sales growing (+80%). Livechat surged mid-2025 then fell back.

Two things jump out. First, fin.ai now generates ~34% of all core web leads — a net-new demand source that didn't exist before May 2025. This inflates total lead volume without changing the underlying buyer profile. Second, Contact Sales leads grew +80% (from ~300/mo to ~500+/mo), likely reflecting CTA changes on the website that offer "Contact Sales" more prominently. Livechat saw a sharp mid-2025 surge (possibly tied to the Fin product launch driving widget traffic) but has since returned closer to baseline.

Meanwhile, Demo volume has been remarkably stable at ~2,800–3,400/mo throughout the period, and Trial volume, while volatile, has not collapsed. The lead mix change is additive (new sources on top of existing), not substitutive (replacing trials with sales leads).

3. Company Size Mix

If we were genuinely moving upmarket, we'd expect the share of Mid-Market + Enterprise (MME) leads to increase at the expense of VSB/SB. This is the most direct test of the "upmarket shift" hypothesis.

Lead Company Size Mix

MME share of core web leads: 13→15%. Slight uptick, not transformative.

Trial Company Size Mix

MME share of trials declining (15%→12%). MM/ENT leaving trial funnel for sales paths.

The answer is clear: the customer profile has barely changed. MME's share of all core web leads moved from 13% to 15% over 18 months — a 2pp shift that's within noise. VSB continues to dominate (~75% of leads), as you'd expect for a product-led motion.

What is interesting is the divergence between leads and trials. MME's share of leads ticked up slightly (13→15%) while their share of trials fell (15%→12%). This means larger companies are increasingly choosing sales-led paths (demo, contact sales) over self-serve trials. This isn't "going upmarket" so much as the funnel is getting better at routing MM/ENT buyers to the right path. The same companies are showing up; they're just being handled differently.

So what? Company size mix is the strongest test of "upmarket" and it shows almost no movement. The trial-to-sales routing shift for larger companies is a positive funnel efficiency signal, but it's not evidence that we're attracting a fundamentally different buyer.

Even if the customer profile hasn't changed, deal sizes might still be growing — which could signal pricing/packaging success or product mix shifts. We look at the full percentile distribution of S2 ACV (ARR at S2 entry) across all core-sourced pipeline.

ACV is growing dramatically — P50 up +260%, P90 up +293% — but critically, the growth is broad-based across all percentiles and both segment groups. If the growth were upmarket-driven, we'd expect the top percentiles (P75/P90) to pull away while P25 stays flat. Instead, even P25 grew from $232 to $1,060 (+357%).

The segment comparison is even more telling: SB P50 grew faster (+314%) than MME P50 (+86%). The gap between the two is narrowing, not widening. This is the opposite of an upmarket story — smaller companies are buying at higher price points, pulling their ACV toward where MM/ENT used to be.

So what? The ACV growth is real but it's a pricing/packaging story, not a customer profile story. The same types of companies are buying more expensive products (likely driven by Fin attach rates and packaging changes). This is good news — it means the growth is repeatable across segments, not dependent on winning a thin slice of upmarket buyers.

Bottom Line
This is a website + pricing story, not an upmarket story. The MQL > Trial divergence is primarily explained by two structural changes: (1) fin.ai created a large new pool of sales-led leads that didn't exist before, and (2) Contact Sales grew as the website offered that path more prominently. Meanwhile, ACV growth is driven by pricing/packaging (likely Fin attach rates and packaging changes), not by winning larger companies — SB deal sizes actually grew faster than MME. The customer profile is essentially unchanged (MME share moved just 2pp in 18 months).
One thing to watch: The routing shift where MM/ENT buyers increasingly prefer sales-led paths over trials (MME trial share falling from 15% to 12%) is worth monitoring. If this continues, it could gradually make the sales-led funnel more upmarket even without a change in overall lead company size mix — a self-reinforcing dynamic where the funnel optimises for the buyers that convert best through it.
